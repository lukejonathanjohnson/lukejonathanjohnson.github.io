information

Ok, so here's how it works:

-we source a t-shirt with a carbon footprint that's 23x smaller than the average high street tee. (650g vs. 15kg)¹

-we work with our partner Sustainable Surf to plant mangroves every time it's sold. (24 for our "One Year" tee and 4609 for our "Lifetime" tee.) We use mangroves as they are 5x more effective at sequestering carbon than tropical land forests². They also reach their full carbon-absorbing potential faster³.

-Over their lifetime, the mangroves absorb the equivalent amount of CO2 emitted by one year of the average EU lifestyle⁴, and a lifetime of the average Australian lifestyle respectively⁵. (We base our “Lifetime” tee on the average Australian lifestyle because Australia has the world’s highest total emissions)⁵.

-built into the number of mangroves we plant is the creation of a carbon-absorbing surplus. This surplus neutralises the very small carbon footprint of the tee. It also neutralises the carbon footprint of our printing, packaging, and shipping too.

About the tee:

another tee is made only from 100 percent organic Indian combed cotton. It is made in facilities powered by green, renewable energy, from low-environmental impact raw materials. 100 percent of tee production undergoes annual audits to comply with the Fair Wear Foundation code of labour practices. The entire production process is controlled and certified in accordance with the latest version of Global Organic Textile Standard. 

We only create another tee when you order it. Whilst this means it takes a little longer to arrive on your doorstep, it also means no materials, energy or resources are wasted.

“One Year” plants 24 mangroves every time it is purchased, offsetting the average annual EU carbon footprint¹. Mangrove Forests are 5x more effective² (and faster³) at sequestering carbon than tropical land forests. A mangrove will absorb 308kg+ CO2 in its lifetime³.

In 2018, Australia had the world's highest CO2 emissions, at 17000kg CO2 per capital⁴. Over 4600 mangroves are planted every time "Lifetime" is purchased, offsetting 83.5 years of this footprint⁵.

why have we made this?

We are on the brink of an unprecedented global catastrophe. Our climate is changing faster than any scientist has predicted. The stakes are real and they are high. Scientists are stressing we have just ten years to dramatically reduce the CO2 in our atmosphere. Failing to do this will see us reach an irreversible tipping point in global temperatures.

We need to suck CO2 out of our atmosphere as fast as possible. We don't have time to wait for Governmental intervention. Preaching and punishing isn't the answer either. We need to own this problem as a populace. We need to create accessible, relatable, repeatable ways for normal people to make a difference. A mangrove will sequester a minimum of 308kg of CO2 in its lifetime³. It does that in just 25 years. Land-based trees, however, can take anywhere from 50-300+ years to reach their full carbon-absorbing potential⁶. (A Giant Sequoia can grow for 3000 years. This is great for long-term carbon absorption. But less helpful to humankind right now.)

Blind consumption has led us to where we are today. If we are to change climate change, we need to buy less and buy smarter. People are not going to stop buying clothes. But we can change the clothes they buy, and the impact that has. The average t-shirt has a carbon footprint of 15kg. Not to mention the True Cost⁸ of fast, unsustainable fashion for people, the planet, and everything living on it.

If even 100 people purchased Another Tee "One Year" instead of the average high-street tee, it would be an immediate saving of 1500kg of CO2. It would also result in the planting of 2400 mangroves, absorbing an additional 739,000kg of CO2³. (That's 160 round-trip flights from London to Hong Kong.)

We want to make the idea of an annual CO2 offset and ocean flora restoration tangible and visible. We believe Another Tee is the way to do it. It's a badge, a signifier between humans - "I'm in this fight too."

-We need to suck CO2 out of our atmosphere as fast as possible. We have less than ten years to do this*. If we don't we will reach an irreversible tipping point in global temperatures.

-A mangrove will sequester a minimum of 308kg of CO2 in its lifetime³. It does that in just 25 years³.

-We need to create ways for normal people to make a difference. People are not going to stop buying clothes. But we can change the clothes they buy, and the impact that has⁸.
